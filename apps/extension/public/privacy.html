TxGuard Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 30, 2026

TxGuard is an AI-powered wallet safety layer for Solana. To provide this protection, the extension inspects transactions and page contexts. This document explains what data we inspect, what we store, and what is sent to our API.

What we inspect (Locally)

The TxGuard content script runs locally in your browser and inspects the following data to detect threats like clickjacking and phishing:

  • DOM Structure: To detect invisible overlays and framing risks.
  • Input Fields: We scan labels and text areas for requests asking for your seed phrase or private key.
  • Wallet-like Controls: We look for buttons mimicking wallet interactions.
  • Page URL & Referrer: Used to assess risk and display in threat reports.
  • Transaction Payload: When you attempt to sign a transaction, we intercept the serialized transaction bytes.

What we send to the API

To analyze risk, we send specific data to the TxGuard API (which you can configure in settings):

  • Serialized Transaction Bytes: The raw transaction payload is sent for simulation and AI analysis. No private keys or signatures are ever sent.
  • Blink URLs: If you interact with a Solana Action (Blink) link, the URL is sent to the API to fetch the transaction payload securely.

What we store (Locally)

We store short-lived, local history in your browser's local storage to provide the recent activity feed in the extension popup. This includes:

  • Recent transaction analysis reports.
  • Recent browser threat signals (like detected overlays or phishing attempts).
  • Your extension settings (API URL, sensitivity, retention days).

This history is cleared automatically based on your retention settings (default 7 days).

What we DO NOT do

  • We do NOT store or transmit your private keys or seed phrase. If we detect a seed phrase prompt on a webpage, we simply block the interaction locally.
  • We do NOT send full page content or your browsing history to our servers.
  • We do NOT store your transaction history remotely. All history is kept in your browser.
  • We do NOT track you across the web or sell your data.

Contact

If you have any questions about our privacy practices, please open an issue in the TxGuard repository.
